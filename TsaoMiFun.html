Tsao Mi Fun


AMy mom's been making me tsao mi fun, in Mandarin, or tsa bi whun, in Taiwanese, since I was a little girl. Tsa bi whun literally translates to 'fried rice noodles'. You'll most likely find all the ingredients at your local supermarket except for the five spice powder, dried Chinese black mushrooms and rice vermicelli which can be found at your local Asian food mart. All the measurements here are pretty much to taste, some people like more pork, some less, some more soy sauce, some less, etc.


Ingredients


½ pound thinly sliced pork loin ¼ cup soy sauce ¼ cup rice wine 1 teaspoon white pepper 1 teaspoon Chinese five-spice powder 1 teaspoon cornstarch 4 dried Chinese black mushrooms 1 (8 ounce) package dried rice vermicelli ¼ cup vegetable oil, divided 2 eggs, beaten ¼ clove garlic, minced 1 tablespoon dried small shrimp 3 carrots, cut into matchstick strips ½ onion, chopped 3 cups bean sprouts 4 leaves napa cabbage, thinly sliced salt to taste 3 sprigs fresh cilantro for garnish
